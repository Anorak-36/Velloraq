# Code Of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want Velloraq to be a practical, respectful open-source security project. Contributors, users, and maintainers are expected to treat each other with patience, professionalism, and good faith.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful in issues, reviews, discussions, and documentation.
- Assume good intent while still being precise about risks and bugs.
- Keep security reports private until coordinated disclosure is complete.
- Give constructive feedback with enough context for others to act.
- Respect different operating systems, experience levels, languages, and deployment environments.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, insults, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private vulnerability details before maintainers have had a chance to respond.
- Sharing credentials, private data, or exploit output from systems you do not own.
- Pressuring contributors to add proprietary lock-in or unsafe behavior.
- Repeated disruption of project work after maintainers ask for a change in behavior.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, reject contributions, or block participants who violate this code. Serious security abuse may be reported to the relevant platform or organization.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns to the maintainers. Report security vulnerabilities through the private process in `SECURITY.md`.
